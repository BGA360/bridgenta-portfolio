# BECC Public Terminology Policy

This policy governs vocabulary selection, spelling compound standards, and proper nouns in German public engineering portfolios.

---

## 1. Classification Categories

* **Category 1: German Public Terminology:** Technical concepts that should be translated into clear standard German to remain accessible to non-technical managers (e.g. `Codegenerierung`).
* **Category 2: Canonical English Terminology:** Standard git parameters, pipeline actions, and architectural layers (e.g. `main branch`, `Repository`, `Preservation Layers`) that should remain in English to avoid translation ambiguity.
* **Category 3: Context-Dependent Terminology:** Terms that use German in structural contexts (e.g., `Arbeitsbereich` in headings) but English in prose contexts (e.g. `Workspace` in paragraphs).

---

## 2. Capitalization and Grammar

* **German Compounds:** Compound nouns imported from English or created in German must follow standard German capitalization rules (e.g. `die Branches`, `der Static-Site-Builder`).
* **Ampersands:** The symbol `&` must not be used in German prose headings. It must be written as `und` unless it forms part of a proprietary name (e.g. `CI/CD`).

---

## 3. Register and Tone
* Maintain a professional **CEFR B2–C1 register** at all times.
* Avoid marketing hype (e.g. "revolutionär", "optimal") and absolute claims ("garantiert", "lückenlos"). Use neutral, evidence-supported engineering verbs.
