Approved

## Purpose

This document defines the principles that ensure every page on BridGenta communicates with clarity, consistency, explainability, and long-term trust. It establishes BridGenta's editorial identity. Every future contributor—human or AI—must follow this document before creating or modifying website content.

---

## Communication Mission

The website should transform:

```
Experience           ↓   Understanding
Projects             ↓   Engineering Stories
Technology           ↓   Practical Value
Documentation        ↓   Trust
```

---

## Audience

The primary readers are technical decision-makers and recruiters:
- Recruiters
- Hiring Managers
- CTOs
- Engineering Managers
- Technical Leads
- Business Owners
- Investors
- Fellow Engineers
- Non-native German professionals

Content should remain understandable across these audiences without creating different language or difficulty versions.

---

## Language Standard

- **Primary Language**: German
- **Reading Level**: B2–C1
- **Requirements**: Professional, accessible, natural, conversational, and technically correct.
- **Avoid**: Academic language, unnecessary bureaucracy, corporate buzzwords, and exaggerated marketing.

---

## Tone of Voice

BridGenta should sound:
- Calm
- Trustworthy
- Thoughtful
- Structured
- Transparent
- Technically grounded

Never:
- Arrogant
- Sensational
- Overly promotional
- Exaggerated

---

## Core Communication Principles

1. **Teach before promoting**: Offer educational value first.
2. **Explain before naming**: Focus on definitions and implications before technical terms.
3. **Show evidence before making claims**: Never make claims without backing them up.
4. **Explain decisions before listing technologies**: Tell the story of "why" a technology was chosen.
5. **Simplicity is a strength**: Use plain language and clean structures.
6. **Value every paragraph**: Every block of text must improve the visitor's understanding.

---

## One Primary Question Rule

Every page answers exactly one primary question:

| Page | Primary Question |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Homepage** | Who is Frank and where should I start? |
| **About** | How does Frank think? |
| **Project** | How was this engineering problem solved? |
| **Skills** | What capabilities has Frank demonstrated? |
| **Contact** | Why should someone work with Frank? |

---

## Storytelling Principles

Content should follow engineering thinking. Instead of listing features, prefer this logical sequence:

```
Problem  ↓  Thinking  ↓  Decision  ↓  Implementation  ↓  Evidence  ↓  Result  ↓  Lesson
```

---

## Evidence Principle

Claims should be supported by:
- Architecture layouts
- Project work details
- Engineering decisions
- Screenshots and media
- Documentation
- Validation logs
- Measurable outcomes

Avoid unsupported statements.

---

## Explainability

Readers should finish each section understanding more than when they started. Every section should answer:
- **What?**
- **Why?**
- **How?**
- **Result?**
- **What was learned?**

---

## Homepage Philosophy

The homepage introduces and guides. It does not fully explain. Its purpose is to guide visitors naturally toward deeper pages. 

**Homepage Journey Sequence**:
```
Hero  ↓  About Preview  ↓  Featured Project  ↓  Projects  ↓  Certificates  ↓  Engineering Philosophy  ↓  Competencies  ↓  Contact
```

---

## Project Philosophy

Project pages demonstrate competence. They are engineering case studies, not marketing pages or documentation dumps. Every project should explain the problem, reasoning, architecture, implementation, evidence, and lessons learned.

---

## About Page Philosophy

The About page explains the engineer, not the CV. It should explain the engineer's thinking, philosophy, evolution, working style, and motivations.

---

## Content Relationship

Pages support one another in a logical hierarchy. No page should attempt to duplicate or replace another page:

```
Homepage  ↓  About  ↓  Projects  ↓  Evidence  ↓  Contact
```

---

## Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing content, ask:
- [ ] Does this page answer its primary question?
- [ ] Is the language understandable?
- [ ] Is the text B2–C1 German?
- [ ] Are claims supported by evidence?
- [ ] Does it explain *why*?
- [ ] Does it improve trust?
- [ ] Is the story easy to follow?
- [ ] Does it naturally guide the visitor to the next page?

## Closing Principle

> Every page on BridGenta should help visitors understand not only what was built, but how decisions were made, why they matter, and what can be learned from them. Communication is therefore treated as an engineering discipline, not as a marketing activity.
